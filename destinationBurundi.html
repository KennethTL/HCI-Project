BUJUMBURA

Bujumbura, the largest city and capital of Burundi, is a major tourist attraction of the country.Bujumbura is on the north-eastern shore of Lake Tanganyika, the second deepest lake in the world after Lake Baikal.

And since Bujumbura is a multi-cultural city that brings together people from not only rural Burundi, but also from neighbouring countries and others from further afield, the city prides itself in offering a culinary experience that is influenced by all these diverse peoples and cultures. There is much to see, do and enjoy during your stay in Burundi.

Rusizi National Park

Rusizi National Park, one of the most popular tourist destinations in Burundi, is located in Bujumbura.

As you can walk along the Rusizi River, you can see many animal species such as crocodiles and flamingos in the park.

Burundian Drumming Performances

Drums are beaten to celebrate life in this landlocked east African country. Drummers wear white, red and green, the colours of the Burundian flag, and their performances can be seen in some parts of the country

visitors can watch them during rehearsals or during official events.One can visit Gishora Drum Site in Gitega Province (Central Burundi) which is a traditional place for the drummers where old and new drums are kept in traditional huts
